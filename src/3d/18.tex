    3.D.18.
    Show that $V$ and $\L(\F, V)$ are isomorphic vector spaces.

    From 3.72,
\begin{align*}
        \op{dim}\L(\F, V) &= (\op{dim}\F)(\op{dim}V)    \\
                          &= 1 \times \op{dim}V         \\
                          &= \op{dim}V .
\end{align*}
    To see that $\op{dim}\F = 1$, consider that any $x \in \F$ can be written as $x1$, regardless of whether $\F$ is $\R$ or $\C$.
    Thus $1$ spans $\F$.
    So $\F$ has a basis of length $1$.

    Thus $V$ and $\L(\F,V)$ are isomorphic because they have the same dimension (from 3.70).
